Hello! I'm Maria Kuosa, soon to be UX designer and experienced digital marketing professional.

Below some of my worksamples featuring UX design and design thinking.

User persona

  • Users personas area great to show what kind of people are going to use the service/product.
  • When creating a persona it can’t just be designers “ideal person” but the facts need to be real and gained through qualitative and/or quantitative research among potential target group.
  • I find this piece of information very useful when making strategic decisions for the service/product.

Empathy map

  • This empathy map was created to ensure that everybody in the project will have as much information as possible of the potential customer/user of the digital product.
  • Empathy maps shows the needs of the customer and brings more insights on the user persona.
  • I find this very useful to create a truly customer centric product/service.

User flow

  • User flows helps to understand the steps customer takes to complete certain action.
  • Usually a service/product is created to make customer/user to complete certain task and user flow makes the purpose clear.
  • This is cost efficient way to explore the service structure and attention goes to the steps and not to the design.
  • I find this very useful step in design process that helps and speeds up the wireframing and mock-up phase of the project.

Iterative design

  • Iterative design process is the way to go in many cases.
  • It is difficult to create a perfect product once but constantly updating the design based on customer/user feedback leads to best results.
  • Starting with pen and paper is still very wise and then moving to more detailed versions.
  • I find it very useful to start with very plain design as it very fast gives an idea if something is possible or not.
  • And when product is launched, design work shouldn’t stop but be constantly updated based on data gathered through various channels.
